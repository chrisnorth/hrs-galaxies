Herschel Reference Survey

The has provided the largest census of dust in local galaxies. You can explore this survey by comparing images of galaxies in the infrared with their images in optical light.

Infrared

In the infrared images, from the Herschel Space Observatory, we see dust in galaxies. The colour of the dust in these images indicates its temperature, with bluer regions being cooler and redder warmer.

Visible light

The visible light images are from the Sloan Digital Sky Survey. Bluer galaxies contain younger, hotter stars, while redder galaxies contain older, cooler stars.
